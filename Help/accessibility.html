Accessibility Options [ Help Index ]

The Accessibility Options is only accessible to the Super Administrator. The Accessibility Options allows the Super Administrator to specify the security restrictions for the other Administrators. It includes the Accessibility Codes which are assigned to every administrator in the system which confines their access rights in the system. The Accessibility Codes are the one which decide the amount of access in the system. It helps to group the Administrators according to their positions or designations.

The Accessibility Options is divided into two parts, one is to change the Designation of the Administrator and the other is to change the Access Codes of the Administrators.

In the first part ie. the upper half contains the Designation on one side and the Administrator on the other side. By pressing the button 'Go' the page not only displays the Accessibility Rights for a particular Designation but also lists the Administrators who have the selected Designation.

On the lower part there is the list of the Access Rights with the priority specified. The priority field indicates the priority given to the particular Designation. The higher the priority the greater the access rights. Also the priority level indicates that all the priority levels below it are also accessible with its own specific access rights.

Also it is important to know that the access rights for the Accessibility Options which is only accessible to the Super Administrator be changed or modified by any means. So access and usage of Accessibility Options is limited to the Super Administrator.
